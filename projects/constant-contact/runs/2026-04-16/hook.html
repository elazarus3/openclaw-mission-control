It's been a while.

If you've been off your GLP-1 medication for a few months — or if you finished a round and aren't sure what comes next — I want you to know: that's more common than you think, and it doesn't mean you've failed.

Life gets complicated. Side effects can be frustrating. Insurance stuff happens. And sometimes you just... fell out of the routine.

Here's the thing: most patients who stop their GLP-1 regain the weight. Not because the medication stopped working — but because the underlying biology didn't change. Your body is still signaling hunger, storing fat, and fighting weight loss the same way it always did.

No lecture. No judgment. Just a conversation about whether getting back on track makes sense for you right now.
